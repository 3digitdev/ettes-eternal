Ettes Eternal attempts to provide scalable stat blocks for four enemy types designed to create consistent, balanced encounters throughout your campaign. Each enemy type serves a specific tactical role and scales automatically with party advancement using Class Tier progression.
- **[[#Mooks]]** provide numerous but fragile opposition
- **[[#Standard Enemies]]** offer reliable moderate threats
- **[[#Lieutenants]]** serve as dangerous mini-bosses requiring coordinated party effort
- **[[#Bosses]]** create climactic encounters with Legendary Actions and Resistances. All statistics are presented as ranges rather than fixed values - adjust within these ranges based on specific enemy concepts, environmental factors, or desired encounter difficulty. The suggested values represent balanced baselines, but feel free to deviate when narrative or tactical considerations call for variation. These guidelines prioritize GM usability over complex customization, providing quick reference tables that scale automatically with party progression while maintaining tactical variety through different enemy type combinations.

Additionally, at the end of this document is a section around special [[#Legendary Actions & Resistances]] for Boss enemies that allow for building truly epic boss battles for your players.

# Enemy Scaling
## Mooks
### Overview
Mooks are numerous, fragile enemies designed to die in a single hit while creating tactical positioning challenges and burning player resources through sheer numbers.
### Stats
- **HP**: 1d4 (dies in 1 hit)
- **Damage**: Party Level ÷ 2, minimum 1
- **Skills**: 4 skills at 30-40 (GM rolls 1d10+30 or chooses)
    - 1 Combat skill (Melee/Ranged/Spellcasting)
    - 1 Physical skill (Agility/Athletics/Endurance)
    - 1 Mental skill (Perception/Willpower)
    - 1 from any category
    - All others at 10
- **Spellcasting**: If chosen
    - Mana = Party Level ÷ 2, minimum 1
    - Max Spell Level = Party Level ÷ 5, minimum 1
    - 1 spell per spell level they can cast
- **Combat**: Full combat rules except no Flow Points

## Standard Enemies
### Overview
Standard Enemies serve as reliable moderate threats that require some tactical consideration but shouldn't overwhelm the party individually.
### Health Points (by Party Class Tier)

| Class Tier | HP Range | Suggested HP |
| ---------- | -------- | ------------ |
| Apprentice | 25-50    | 35           |
| Journeyman | 35-70    | 50           |
| Expert     | 45-90    | 65           |
| Master     | 70-135   | 100          |
| Legendary  | 90-175   | 130          |
### Skills
**Distribution:**
- 1 Combat Skill (Melee/Ranged/Spellcasting): 40-50
- 1 Physical Skill (Agility/Athletics/Endurance): 40-50
- 1 Mental Skill (Perception/Willpower): 40-50
- 2-3 other skills from any category: ~30
- All remaining skills: 10
**Scaling:** Skills in the 40-50 range should be adjusted based on party level within that range
### Damage Output

| Party Class Tier | Damage Formula | Average Damage¹ |
| ---------------- | -------------- | --------------- |
| Apprentice       | 1d4 + modifier | 6               |
| Journeyman       | 1d8 + modifier | 8.5             |
| Expert           | 1d8 + modifier | 8.5             |
| Master           | 2d8 + modifier | 13              |
| Legendary        | 3d8 + modifier | 17.5            |

_¹ Assumes ~+4 skill modifier from 40-50 combat skill range_
### Combat Rules
- **No Flow Points**
- All standard combat rules apply (reactions, escalating costs, etc.)
### Special Abilities
Standard Enemies typically have **0-1 simple special abilities** based on the enemy type to distinguish them from Mooks

# Legendary Actions & Resistances
## What Are Legendary Actions & Resistances?
Legendary Actions and Legendary Resistances are special abilities exclusive to Boss-tier enemies that solve the fundamental challenge of single powerful opponents facing an entire party of adventurers. Without these mechanics, even the most dangerous boss would be overwhelmed by sheer action economy - taking one turn while 4-6 player characters act multiple times per round.

These systems ensure that boss encounters feel appropriately climactic and challenging throughout the entire fight, rather than becoming trivial once players coordinate their tactics.
## Why These Systems Exist
### The Action Economy Problem
In standard combat, a single boss enemy faces a severe disadvantage:
- **4-6 player characters** each taking full turns
- **Boss gets 1 turn** per round, regardless of party size
- **Players coordinate** attacks, buffs, and tactical positioning
- **Result**: Even powerful bosses become manageable through sheer numerical advantage
### The Solution
Legendary Actions and Resistances address this by:
- **Increasing boss activity** throughout each round
- **Preventing easy spell-based victories** through resistances
- **Maintaining dramatic tension** from start to finish
- **Creating strategic decisions** for both players and GMs

---
# Legendary Actions
## Core Mechanics
### Action Pool
Bosses gain **3 Legendary Action points** that refresh at the start of their turn each round.  All Legendary Actions cost **1 point each**, unless they specify otherwise.
### Timing Restrictions
- **Only usable** at the end of another creature's turn
- **One Legendary Action per turn maximum** (cannot interrupt the same character multiple times)
- **Must declare immediately** when another character ends their turn
## Design Guidelines for GMs
### Special Abilities for Legendary Actions
When designing boss-specific Legendary Actions:
- **Make them interesting**: Consider effects like area damage, status effects, summoning, powerful attacks, or major battlefield changes
- **Maintain theme**: Abilities should reflect the boss's nature and combat style
### Timing Considerations
- **Interrupt momentum**: Use after successful player actions to maintain pressure
- **Respond tactically**: Position after players commit to strategies
- **Create urgency**: Keep players engaged even when it's not the boss's turn
### Balancing Usage
- **Don't spam attacks**
- **Respond to player tactics**: Use abilities that counter current player strategies
- **Maintain narrative flow**: Choose actions that enhance the story drama

# Legendary Resistances
## Core Mechanics
### Resistance Pool
Bosses gain **3-5 Legendary Resistances** per encounter (exact number based on boss tier and expected encounter length).
### Trigger Condition
**Only activates** when the boss loses an Opposed Check against a spell effect. This includes:
- **Direct spell attacks** that require Opposed Checks
- **Save-or-suck effects** like charm, paralysis, or transformation
- **Debuffing spells** that impose status effects
- **Special Opposed actions** like Grapple, Trip, etc.
### Resolution Process
1. **Boss loses** the Opposed Check normally
2. **GM declares** use of Legendary Resistance
3. **Resistance is expended** (announce remaining count)
4. **Boss wins** the Opposed Check by exactly the minimum needed
5. **Spell effect fails** as if the boss had won normally
## Critical Limitations
### What Legendary Resistances DO NOT Affect
- **Non-magical damaging attacks** from any source
- **Critical hits** from any source (preserve awesome player moments)
- **Damage spells** that hit automatically
### GM Guidelines for Fair Play
#### Transparency Requirements
- **Roll openly** when using Legendary Resistances
- **Announce usage clearly**: "The boss uses a Legendary Resistance"
- **Track publicly**: "The boss has 2 Legendary Resistances remaining"
- **Never hide the mechanic** - players should understand what's happening
#### Strategic Usage
- **Save for important moments**: Don't waste on minor effects
- **Create dramatic tension**: Use when spell would end encounter prematurely
- **Respect player creativity**: Allow some clever spells to succeed
- **Never block critical hits**: Preserve epic player moments
#### Narrative Integration
- **Describe the resistance**: "The charm magic washes over the dragon's ancient mind without effect"
- **Show limitation**: Make it clear when resistances are depleted
- **Build anticipation**: Players should feel progress as resistances are expended
# Implementation of Legendary Actions
## For Game Masters
### Pre-Combat Preparation
- **Determine resistance count** based on expected encounter length
- **Plan special abilities** suitable for Legendary Actions
- **Consider boss motivations** for when to use each resistance
### During Combat
- **Track usage openly** with visible counters or notes
- **Respond to player tactics** rather than following rigid patterns
- **Maintain encounter pacing** through strategic ability usage
- **Preserve dramatic moments** by avoiding resistance overuse
## For Players
### Strategic Considerations
- **Legendary Actions increase boss activity** throughout each round
- **Resistances protect** against save-or-suck spells but are limited
- **Critical hits bypass** Legendary Resistances entirely
- **Resistance depletion** opens up spell-based tactics later in fight
### Tactical Approaches
- **Force early resistances** with less critical spells
- **Save powerful effects** until resistances are depleted
- **Focus on reliable damage** rather than single-effect victories
- **Coordinate timing** to maximize pressure between resistances
